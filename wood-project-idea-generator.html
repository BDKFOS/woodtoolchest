What kinds of projects are included?

The generator leans toward practical woodworking builds that make sense in a home shop. That includes small furniture, jigs, shop helpers, utility pieces, and skill-building projects.

  • cutting boards and serving boards

Why this is useful

A lot of shop momentum gets lost in indecision. A simple idea generator helps narrow the options and turns “I should build something” into an actual next step.

Best mindset: do not treat the result like a command. Treat it like a prompt. If one click gives you a better version of an idea you already had, that is still a win.

How to use the result well

Match it to your current energy

If the generator gives you a medium or advanced project and you are tired, scale it down. A simpler build you finish is more useful than a more impressive one you abandon.

Use the wood suggestion as a starting point

The listed wood is there to make the project feel concrete. You can swap species based on what you already have in the shop.

Pay attention to the shop note

The note is there to point you toward the real challenge; glue-up, layout, milling, repeatability, or surface cleanup.
